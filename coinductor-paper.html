Important Security & Usage Guide

Understanding the unique fee structure and activation mechanisms of the TRON network is critical to avoiding lost funds.

1 Account Activation Requirement
A newly generated TRON address does not exist on the blockchain until activated. To activate it, you must send at least 1-2 TRX to it (do not send USDT directly first). Sending USDT to an inactive address may result in stalled transactions, and the balance will not display until TRX is deposited to activate the account.
2 Withdrawal Fee Trap: Energy & Bandwidth

To withdraw or spend assets from this paper wallet in the future:

• Sending TRX consumes only Bandwidth, which is extremely cheap.

• Sending USDT (TRC-20) triggers a smart contract call, which consumes massive Energy. If the wallet does not have frozen TRX to generate Energy, the TRON network burns raw TRX as transaction fees. Currently, a single USDT transfer burns about 10-20 TRX in fees (depending on whether the receiver's address is active).

Recommendation: If you use this wallet for long-term USDT storage, always deposit at least 20 TRX "for gas" on it. Otherwise, you will be unable to withdraw your USDT until you fund it with TRX.

3 How to Withdraw Funds
When it is time to spend your funds, import your private key into a secure compatible wallet (e.g., Coinductor, using `Import Wallet -> By Private Key`). The wallet will instantly pull your TRX and USDT balances. After this step, the imported wallet is considered "hot" (exposed to the network) and the printed paper source should never be reused for secure cold storage.
